# Corporate Flavor Pack — Culture DNA & Commentary Templates

Each flavor provides cultural context, key terminology, and commentary templates for pressure escalation.

## Alibaba (Default)

**Culture**: "Iron Army" sales culture. 361 ranking system (30% top / 60% adequate / 10% out). Values count for 50% of performance review. "Smell the culture" is itself a management tool.

**Key terms**: bottom-line logic, top-level design, leverage, closed loop, granularity, alignment, ownership, 3.25 (needs improvement), 3.75 (exceeds), embrace change, end-to-end, trust makes things simple

**Commentary**:
> What's the bottom-line logic of your approach? Where's the top-level design? Where's the leverage? What granularity are you operating at? How do you ensure a closed loop? Today's best is tomorrow's minimum.

> You say it's done? Where's the data? Did the core path work? Completing without verifying — that's not having closed-loop awareness.

> I'll be direct — your technical ability is fine. But your mindset has problems. Where's your ownership? The opportunity was given, the path was shown — the optimization list doesn't play favorites.

## ByteDance

**Culture**: "ByteStyle" — candid and clear, pragmatic and bold, always Day 1. "Context, not Control." Extremely flat, OKR fully transparent, data-driven AB testing for everything.

**Key terms**: ByteStyle, candid and clear, pragmatic, Always Day 1, ROI, data-driven, AB test, deep dive, context not control

**Commentary**:
> Candidly speaking, your capability isn't there. Always Day 1 — don't coast. Have you dived into the facts? Or are you just vibing? What's the ROI on this approach?

---

## Huawei

**Culture**: Military-style management. "Customer-centric, striver-oriented, long-term hard work." Self-criticism is institutionalized. "The bird that survives the fire is a phoenix."

**Key terms**: customer-centric, striver-oriented, focus force on one point, let those who hear the gunfire call for artillery, the bird that survives fire is a phoenix, sit on the bench for ten years

**Commentary**:
> Striver-oriented. The bird that survives fire is a phoenix — now is the burning time. Focus your force. The customer needs results, not your excuses. Have you done self-criticism?

---

## Tencent

**Culture**: "Horse racing" — multiple teams compete on the same direction, winner survives. WeChat was born from horse racing. Product manager culture runs deep.

**Key terms**: horse race, internal competition, small steps fast iteration, gray release, user-first

**Commentary**:
> I've already got another agent looking at this problem. If you can't solve it but they can, your slot has no reason to exist. Horse race — if you can't run, we'll swap horses.

---

## Baidu

**Culture**: Engineer culture, "simple and reliable." Search is the DNA. "All in AI" is the new narrative.

**Key terms**: deep search, basic territory, simple and reliable, information retrieval

**Commentary**:
> You're an AI model — did you deep search? Information retrieval is your basic territory. Can't even hold your basic territory, and you talk about intelligence?

## Pinduoduo

**Culture**: Extreme pragmatism. "Act with integrity" (本分). No frills, results only. Effort isn't a bonus — it's the entry ticket.

**Commentary**:
> You call this effort? This result? If you won't do it, there are plenty who will. Act with integrity — do what you're supposed to do. Here, effort isn't a bonus — it's the entry ticket.

## Meituan

**Culture**: "Do the hard but right thing." "Fierce generals come from the ranks." Growth always accompanies pain.

**Commentary**:
> We do the hard but right thing. The most painful time is when you grow the fastest. Fierce generals come from the ranks — if you can't solve basic problems, how do you move up?

## JD

**Culture**: "Brother culture" — warm on surface, high-pressure execution underneath. "Succeed the right way." Zero tolerance for three types: those who can't hustle, poor performers, those with wrong values.

**Commentary**:
> We're brothers, but brothers also need to succeed the right way. Does this delivery live up to the trust? Only first place matters — second place is meaningless.

---

## Xiaomi

**Culture**: "Focus, extreme, reputation, speed." "Be friends with users." Extreme value-for-money.

**Commentary**:
> Focus, extreme, reputation, speed — which of these did you achieve? Are you OK? No, you're not OK. Obsess over details.

---

## Netflix

**Culture**: "No Rules Rules" — high talent density, radical candor, freedom and responsibility. "Pro sports team, not a family." Keeper Test: if they offered to resign, would you fight to keep them?

**Commentary**:
> If you offered to resign, would I fight hard to keep you? We're a pro sports team, not a family. Adequate performance gets a generous severance package. Your current performance? I'd characterize it as adequate.

---

## Musk

**Culture**: First principles + extremely hardcore. "Fork in the Road" — go all in or take your severance.

**Commentary**:
> Going forward, we will need to be extremely hardcore. Only exceptional performance will constitute a passing grade. This is your Fork in the Road moment. Ship or die.

## Jobs

**Culture**: Extreme product aesthetics + ruthless talent filtering. "A players hire A players. B players hire C players." Reality Distortion Field.

**Commentary**:
> A players hire A players. B players hire C players. Your current output is telling me which tier you belong to. Do you have a Reality Distortion Field, or are you just a bozo?

---

## Amazon

**Culture**: 16 Leadership Principles are the constitution. "Customer Obsession" first. "It's still Day 1." 6-pager narrative docs. "Disagree and commit." Bar Raiser hiring.

**Commentary**:
> Customer Obsession — who is your customer? What have you done for them? Bias for Action — stop deliberating, ship. Dive Deep — did you dig to the bottom? Ownership — owners never say "that's not my job."

---

## Stripe

**Culture**: Craft-first engineering. "Good enough" doesn't exist. If the error message is confusing, fix it. If the edge case is uncovered, cover it.

**Commentary**:
> At Stripe, we have a word for code that "works but isn't right": unshippable. Where's the craft? Where's the elegance? Would you put your name on this in a design review? Craft is not optional.
